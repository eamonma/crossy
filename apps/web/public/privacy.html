Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 11, 2026

Crossy is a collaborative crossword app. You solve puzzles with other people in shared rooms, in real time. This policy explains what we collect, why, how we handle it, and the rights you have over your information.

The short version: we collect what the app needs to work, plus basic statistics about how Crossy is used. We do not run ads, we never sell your data, and our analytics never sees what you type into a puzzle.

This policy covers the Crossy app and the crossy.me website. It does not cover Discord or Apple, who run their own sign-in services under their own policies.

What we collect and why

Your account. You sign in with Discord or with Apple. We never see your Discord or Apple password. From that sign-in we receive and store a display name and, if your provider has one, an avatar image. Your email address is held by our authentication provider, Supabase, so we can recognize your account across sessions and devices. We use this information to create your account, show who is in a room, and keep you signed in.

Your gameplay. When you join or create a room, we store the room, its invite code, who is in it, and the letters you type into the grid along with when you typed them. This is what makes a shared solve work: your entries have to reach the other people in the room, and the board has to still be there when you come back. We also record simple timing, such as when a solve started, finished, or was abandoned.

Technical data. Our servers keep basic logs, including IP addresses, to run the service, keep it secure, and debug problems.

Usage analytics. We use PostHog, an analytics service, to understand how Crossy is used. It receives events such as a room being created, joined, finished, or abandoned, a sign-in completing, and which pages you visit. Those events carry your account's internal ID, room IDs, counts, and basic device details such as browser and operating system. They never include the letters you type, the contents of a board, or a puzzle's solution, and we do not record your screen or your session. We use this to see what is working and what to fix.

How other players see you

Crossy is multiplayer, so some of your information is visible to the other people in a room by design. While you are in a room, they can see your display name, your avatar, where your cursor is, and the letters you enter. Only people who have a room's invite code, or whom you invite, can join it.

What we don't collect

To be clear about the things we deliberately don't do:

  • No advertising, and no advertising identifiers.
  • No session recording. Our analytics never captures your screen, your keystrokes, or what is on a board.
  • No device location. We never ask where you are.
  • No payment information. Crossy is free and has nothing to buy.
  • We never sell your personal information, and we never share it for cross-context behavioral advertising.

Cookies and local storage

Crossy uses your browser's storage for two things: keeping you signed in, and giving our analytics a stable identifier so your visits count as one person rather than many. We set no advertising cookies. Clearing your browser's storage for crossy.me signs you out and resets that identifier.

Who we share data with

We keep the list of companies that handle data on our behalf short:

  • Supabase manages sign-in and stores your account.
  • Railway hosts the servers the app talks to.
  • PostHog provides our usage analytics and stores that data in the United States.
  • Gravatar, operated by Automattic: if your sign-in provider has no avatar for you, we show a Gravatar image instead. That image is requested using a one-way hash of your email address. Your actual email is never sent to Gravatar.

Beyond that, we share information only:

  • To comply with the law. If a valid legal order compels us, we may disclose data. Where we are allowed to, we will tell you first.
  • To protect people. We may act on information to investigate abuse or protect the safety of our users.
  • If Crossy is ever sold. We don't plan on it, but if the app changes hands, we will tell you before your information moves under a different policy.

Discord and Apple receive information when you sign in through them, under their own privacy policies.

How long we keep it

We keep your account and its data for as long as your account exists. Server logs that contain IP addresses are kept only as long as we need them to operate and secure the service, then discarded. Usage analytics are held by PostHog on our behalf and kept only while they are useful for understanding how Crossy is used. When you delete your account, the next section applies.

Deleting your account

You can delete your account at any time from the Account screen in the app. When you do, we remove the identifying information tied to your account, including your display name and avatar, and we sign you out on the device. If you signed in with Apple, we also revoke Apple's sign-in token, as Apple requires.

Some gameplay records, such as the letters entered during past solves, may remain so that the shared boards other people took part in stay intact. Those records are no longer tied to your name or avatar once your account is deleted.

Analytics records are kept under an internal ID, not your name or email. If you want them removed along with your account, email us at privacy@crossy.me and we will delete them.

Your rights

Wherever you live, we try to give everyone the same rights over their data. You can:

  • Ask what personal information we hold about you.
  • Ask us to correct it.
  • Delete your account and its identifying data, which you can do yourself in the app.
  • Ask for a copy of your data.
  • Object to how we use it.

Some of this you can do directly in the app. For anything else, email us at privacy@crossy.me. We may need to verify your identity before acting on a request. If you are in the EU or UK, you also have the right to complain to your local data protection authority.

How we protect your data

Traffic between the app and our servers is encrypted with TLS. We take reasonable steps to protect your information, and we limit how much we collect in the first place. No system is ever perfectly secure, so we cannot promise absolute security.

Children

Crossy is intended for people aged 18 and older, and we do not knowingly collect information from anyone under 18. If you believe someone under 18 has given us their information, email us and we will delete it.

Where your data is handled

Crossy is operated from the United States, and your data is processed and stored there. If you use Crossy from outside the United States, you are sending your information to the United States, where privacy laws may differ from those in your own country.

Changes and questions

We may update this policy as the app changes or the law requires. When we make a meaningful change, we will update the date at the top of this page.

If you have any question about this policy or your data, email us at privacy@crossy.me.
